Who is Pele?

Edson Arantes do Nascimento, born on October 23, 1940, in Três Corações, Brazil, emerged from humble beginnings. Bestowed with the nickname “Pelé” during his youth, the moniker, though born of a mispronunciation, would become synonymous with football greatness.

Raised in poverty, Pelé’s early years involved honing his skills with a grapefruit as a makeshift ball. His innate talent shone through, and by 15, he joined Santos FC, marking the commencement of a legendary career.

Pele Career

Pelé’s professional journey ignited with Santos FC, where he showcased unparalleled prowess, winning the Intercontinental Cup and Copa Libertadores. Despite numerous offers from European giants, Pelé remained loyal to Santos. His transcendent impact reached its pinnacle when he signed with the New York Cosmos in 1975, earning a groundbreaking $4.5 million contract, equivalent to $25 million today.

The football luminary’s World Cup triumphs are etched in history, with victories in 1958, 1962, and 1970, solidifying his status as a national hero in Brazil. Pelé’s goal-scoring prowess remains unparalleled, with 541 goals in the top division and a staggering 1297 goals across all tournaments.

Pele Puma Contract

Pelé’s financial acumen matched his on-field brilliance. His association with Puma in the 1970s, marked by a $120,000 endorsement deal, showcased the football legend’s impact even off the pitch. The iconic “shoe-tying” incident during the 1970 World Cup semifinal further solidified Pelé’s marketability.

Pele Ambassadorial Roles

Post-retirement, Pelé seamlessly transitioned into ambassadorial roles, championing causes as a UN ambassador for ecology and the environment. However, controversies, including a 2001 corruption scandal and disputes with FIFA, added complexity to his legacy.

Pele Wife

Pelé’s personal life unfolded with three marriages, notably to Rosemeri dos Reis Cholbi and Assiria Lemos Seixas. His seven children bore witness to the football legend’s complex personal journey. Real estate ventures, including a New York City apartment and a waterfront mansion in East Hampton, further showcased Pelé’s multifaceted life.

Pele Cause of Death

Pelé’s passing in 2022 marked the end of an era, but his legacy lives on. In March 2023, Kihali Pelé Stadium in Rwanda was named in his honor, underscoring the enduring impact of a football icon.
