She Became James Caan’s Wife in 1990

James Caan and Ingrid Hajek were married from September 1990 to March 1994. Their wedding took place on September 9, 1990, on Regentsea, a boat in Marina Del Rey’s harbor. It was a private event attended by a few family members and friends who came to witness the union.

For Caan, it was his third marriage, and he was 49 years old at the time. Ingrid, on the other hand, was going into matrimony for the first time, and she was 29 years old.

The generational gap between the two did nothing to stop their love from growing. Just a year into the marriage, they welcomed their first and only child as a couple. Their son was named Alexander James Caan, and he was born in 1991. By the time their son was three years old, the marriage crashed.

James Caan was Already Famous When Ingrid Married Him

James Caan was active as an actor from 1960 until his death in 2022. Thus, he was already a star by the time he met and married Ingrid in 1990. His career breakthrough came with his performance as Sonny Corleone in Coppola’s The Godfather (1972). The role earned him Academy and Golden Globe Award nominations for Best Supporting Actor. He later reprised his role in The Godfather Part II (1974).

Other notable projects under his belt include Brian’s Song (1971), The Gambler (1974), Funny Lady (1975), Cinderella Liberty (1973), Rollerball (1975), A Bridge Too Far (1977), Comes a Horseman (1978), Chapter Two (1979), The Yards (2000), City of Ghosts (2002), Dogville (2003), Elf (2003), and Get Smart (2008).

With regard to his talent, Caan received many award recognitions in his lifetime, including an Academy Award, two Primetime Emmy Awards, and four Golden Globe Awards. He also bagged his own star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1978.

Why Did James Caan and Ingrid Hajek Get Divorced?

On March 29, 1994, Ingrid and James Caan went their separate ways, ending their marriage of barely four years. The reason for their divorce has remained undisclosed as the former couple has since moved on with their lives.

A 1994 interview with former Hollywood madam Heidi Fleiss threw light on possible reasons the marriage between Ingrid and James ended. Speaking to Vanity Fair at the time, Fleiss claimed she was having an affair with James Caan in 1992, the same time he was still married to Ingrid Hajek. Her claims further revealed she visited the actor while he was working in Texas on the set of Flesh and Bones.

Responding to Heidi Fleiss’s interview, James Caan insisted that their relationship was only platonic with no strings attached. However, there is nothing to show if the said relationship had anything to do with his divorce from Ingrid.

After ending his marriage with Ingrid Hajek, Caan went ahead to marry Linda Stokes on October 7, 1995, and they had two sons. His union with Linda Stokes suffered the same fate as the previous three. The actor didn’t get married again until his death on July 6, 2022.

James Caan was 82 years old when he died in Los Angeles after getting admitted to Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center for a heart attack. His death was caused by complications from coronary artery disease, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, and congestive heart failure.

Caan was later buried at Eden Memorial Park Cemetery with tributes from fellow Hollywood stars like Robert Duvall, Robert De Niro, and Al Pacino among others.
