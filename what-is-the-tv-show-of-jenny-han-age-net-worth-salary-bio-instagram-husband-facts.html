Professional Career | Work and Job | What is she doing for living?

Jenny Han wrote her first book while still in college, the kid’s book Shug, which was released in 2006. The protagonist of the book is a 12-year-old girl named Annemarie Wilcox who is attempting to survive junior high school’s challenges. Later, Han spent her summer breaks working on a young adult romance trilogy about a girl’s coming-of-age.

She worked on three Simon & Schuster-published novels from 2009 to 2011 with the titles The Summer I Turned Pretty, It’s Not Summer Without You, and We’ll Always Have Summer. The trilogy also becomes one of the New York Times Best Sellers.

Her second young adult trilogy, which she co-wrote with Siobhan Vivian, was later released. It appeared in Burn for Burn’s publishing from 2012. Three high school girls are described as pursuing vengeance in their island village in the book, which also has magical and romantic overtones.

In 2014, she also published the young adult romance book To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before. The story revolves around high school senior Lara Jean Song Covey, whose world is turned upside down when the letters she wrote to her five previous crushes are accidentally sent to them.

In addition, the novel was picked up for a movie adaptation only a few weeks after it was released. The movie version of the book, which starred actress Lana Condor in the title role, started filming in July 2017 and was released by Netflix in August 2018 to generally favorable reviews.

Han also makes a brief appearance in the movie. P.S. I Still Love You and To All the Boys: Always and Forever, the sequel, were published in 2020 and 2021, respectively.
