The Achievements of Jeff Lerner

Jeff Lerner has made a profound impact on the tech business landscape through his entrepreneurial endeavors. Notably, he has taken on roles as an angel investor and venture capitalist, actively contributing to the growth of digital marketing and online advertising.

In 1995, Lerner co-founded his inaugural venture, i-Cube, a pioneering company in the development of ad servers for online publishers. This early success culminated in the acquisition of i-Cube by the media conglomerate WPP plc in 2000.

Undeterred by his initial success, Lerner forged ahead, founding eBureau in 2001. This innovative firm specialized in predictive analytics software, particularly utilized by insurance companies to calculate auto insurance premiums. Lerner’s tenacity bore fruit in 2008 when Experian acquired eBureau.

2009 marked the inception of another notable venture, AdRoll, co-founded by Jeff Lerner. AdRoll emerged as a prominent retargeting platform, serving the needs of over 20,000 global businesses. Recognized as one of Time magazine’s “50 Best Websites of 2011,” AdRoll’s excellence eventually led to its acquisition by the Japanese internet giant Rakuten in 2017, commanding a staggering $850 million.

Beyond his entrepreneurial ventures, Lerner holds the distinction of being a founding partner at Battery Ventures, a prestigious venture capital firm renowned for its investments in cutting-edge technology companies. His contributions extend to several public and private companies, including Clickable (subsequently acquired by Google), KISSmetrics (acquired by Neil Patel), and OpenX (which went public with an IPO in 2014).
