What is Kyle Chandler’s Net Worth and Salary?

Kyle Chandler is an American actor with a net worth of $12 million. He is most famous for his performances in “Super 8,” “Early Edition,” “Grey’s Anatomy,” and “Friday Night Lights.” Chandler won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series in 2011 for his role as Coach Eric Taylor in “Friday Night Lights.”

Kyle Chandler’s Early Life

Kyle Chandler was born in Buffalo, New York in 1965 and grew up with three siblings. His father was a pharmaceutical sales representative and his mother was a dog breeder who raised Great Danes for show dogs. Chandler spent his childhood traveling to dog shows and helping out at his parents’ kennel. He attended George Walton Academy in Monroe, Georgia, where he played football until he quit his freshman year following his father’s death. Chandler then joined the theater program at Walton and went on to study drama at the University of Georgia. However, he dropped out of college in 1998, just seven credits shy of a bachelor’s degree in drama, to pursue a television deal.

Kyle Chandler’s Career Highlights

Kyle Chandler’s television film debut was in 1988, and he studied acting with Milton Katselas. He appeared in “Tour of Duty” and “Homefront,” and made his Broadway debut in “Picnic” in 1994. Chandler’s breakthrough role came in 1996 when he played the lead character in CBS’s “Early Edition,” for which he won the Saturn Award for Best Actor on Television. He became a household name in 2006 when he starred in “Friday Night Lights,” which earned critical acclaim and a cult following after it hit Netflix. Chandler won an Emmy for Outstanding Actor in a Drama Series for his role in the show.

After “Friday Night Lights,” Chandler focused on the big screen, appearing in films such as “Super 8,” “Argo,” “Zero Dark Thirty,” “The Wolf of Wall Street,” and “The Spectacular Now.” In 2015, he returned to TV with the Netflix series “Bloodline,” for which he was nominated for an Emmy Award. Chandler also appeared in “Manchester by the Sea,” “The Vanishing of Sidney Hall,” and “Game Night.” He played a role in “Godzilla: King of the Monsters” and is set to reprise the role in “Godzilla vs. Kong.” Chandler recently joined the cast of Netflix’s “Slumberland.”

Personal Life and Philanthropy

Kyle Chandler has been married to Kathryn Macquarrie since 1995, and they have two daughters, Sydney and Sawyer. The couple met at a dog park in the mid-’90s.

In 2007, Chandler and his family moved to a 33-acre spread 30 miles southwest of Austin, Texas, where they own several animals. Chandler’s mother, who had Alzheimer’s, lived with the family toward the end of her life and passed away in 2014.

Chandler serves as a volunteer firefighter in his small community of Dripping Springs, Texas. He also participates in an annual charity golf tournament to raise funds for football players living with spinal cord injuries.
