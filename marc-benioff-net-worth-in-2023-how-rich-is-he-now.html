Who is Marc Benioff?

Marc Benioff is a renowned American entrepreneur, billionaire, and philanthropist, known for his leadership as the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Salesforce, a global software company. Born in 1964 in San Francisco, California, he graduated from the University of Southern California with a degree in computer science.

After starting his career as a software developer at Oracle Corporation, Benioff co-founded Salesforce in 1999. Under his guidance, Salesforce has grown into one of the world's largest software companies, serving over 150,000 customers in more than 190 countries. His vision transformed the customer relationship management (CRM) industry by making CRM software accessible and affordable for businesses of all sizes.

Beyond his professional success, Marc Benioff is a dedicated philanthropist. Alongside his wife, Lynne, he has contributed substantial sums to various causes, including education, healthcare, and environmental conservation. They established the Benioff Foundation, supporting numerous social impact initiatives.

Benioff's commitment extends to advocating for social justice and environmental sustainability. He's spoken out against discrimination, championing LGBT rights, and urged businesses to address climate change. His membership in the World Economic Forum's Global Future Council on the Future of Consumption underscores his commitment to global issues.

Notable achievements include being named one of Time magazine's 100 Most Influential People in the World, receiving the EY Entrepreneur of the Year Award, and being recognized as the World's Most Innovative CEO by Fast Company. He is also an author, with his book "Trailblazer: The Power of Business as the Greatest Platform for Change." Marc Benioff's legacy is that of a visionary leader who has significantly shaped the technology industry while using his wealth and influence to create a positive impact on society and the world at large.
