Getting ready for a trip but unsure of what deodorant to take? Our handy buyer’s guide will answer all your travel size deodorant questions and give you some tips on how to purchase the right one for you. Read on to make an informed decision. 

Why Should You Buy Travel Size Deodorants?

Frequent travelers need smaller-sized products to take with them wherever they go. But there are several requirements for skin care and cosmetics that you can’t overlook. During holidays and trips abroad, deodorants are a must-have. Most choose travel size versions of these items since they’re convenient to carry and apply while traveling. These small deodorants are often TSA-approved and simple to use. They come in scented and unscented forms, both of which include antiperspirant elements. All in all, a travel-friendly deodorant helps you stay smelling fresh while exploring the world.

Factors To Consider When Buying Travel Size Deodorant 

Ingredients

If you’re looking for the most effective deodorant, make sure it’s made entirely of natural ingredients. Aluminum is a chemical used in conventional deodorants that blocks your sweat glands, so be sure it isn’t in your travel size deodorant to prevent suffering from rashes or allergic reactions.

Destination

You should also consider where you’re traveling because not all destinations will have a store nearby. Plus, supermarkets near popular destinations have either low-quality products or the prices are too high. That’s why it’s better to take your favorite deodorant with you. Also remember that while traveling in certain regions — such as across Asia or Africa — you may have trouble finding your preferred mainstream products.

What Should You Look For in a Travel Size Deodorant?

Size

If you want to take your deodorant on the airplane, make sure it is 3.8 ounces or less in size. Having a compact travel size deodorant is useful for all types of travel, not just traveling by air as it can fit easily into your bag without taking up too much room. 

Roll-on or aerosol spray

Although the ingredients in both these deodorant types are almost the same, some may prefer roll-on sticks due to the solid texture while others may be inclined towards aerosol sprays due to the hygiene factor. If you have sensitive skin, a roll-on stick is the better choice because it’s less likely to sting or irritate your skin. Sprays may have ingredients that irritate your skin, cause allergic reactions, and even result in breathing problems. Roll-ons are also more organic than aerosol spray cans because they are biodegradable.

Duration

While most deodorants provide 24-hour coverage, some companies claim to keep the user dry and smelling good for 36 to 48 hours. You can also get extra-strong antiperspirant pads that provide up to seven days of coverage for those who sweat a lot.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Travel Size Deodorants

Most individuals need to use deodorant once a day, while others need to reapply it again throughout the day to keep excess perspiration at bay. We recommend getting a natural, clinically-approved, and dermatologically-tested deodorant to treat your sweat glands naturally.

It’s better to opt for hypoallergenic deodorants if you’re prone to skin-related diseases or allergies. You can find hypersensitive products with skin-soothing components that offer a cooling effect to soothe irritated skin. As it's generally the scent that causes discomfort, you might be happier with an unscented alternative.
